Lesson 14: Time Expressions + Days of the Week

Estimated Time: 25 minutes

Objective: Learn time expressions (now, tomorrow) and days of the week to discuss travel schedules.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

  • Explain time expressions for planning (e.g., I go tomorrow).
  • Introduce days of the week for scheduling.
  • Preview combining with to go and destinations.

2. Grammar Focus – Time Expressions

Time Expressions:

  • now – ahora
  • today – hoy
  • tomorrow – mañana
  • the day after tomorrow – pasado mañana
  • tonight – esta noche

Days of the Week:

SpanishEnglish
el lunesMonday
el martesTuesday
el miércolesWednesday
el juevesThursday
el viernesFriday
el sábadoSaturday
el domingoSunday

Structure: Subject + verb + time/day + destination.

  • I go to the beach tomorrow. – Voy a la playa mañana.
  • We are in the museum on Monday. – Estamos en el museo el lunes.

Key Rule: Days are masculine. Use el before days; no article for time expressions. Task: Change I go to the square today to You go to the museum on Friday.

3. Vocabulary – Time/Planning

SpanishEnglish
el horariothe schedule
la semanathe week

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

Say: I go to the park tomorrow. We are in the square on Saturday.
Ask: Where do you go on Monday? → Pause for: I go to the museum on Monday.
Goal: Recognize time expressions and days.

5. Reading + Matching Task

  • I go to the museum on Tuesday →
  • I am in the beach today →
  • It’s a new schedule →
  • Identify: verb, time, destination in "We go to the mountain on Friday".

6. Speaking Prompt

  • Where do you go tomorrow? → I go to the beach tomorrow.
  • Say you’re going to a park on Friday → I go to a park on Friday.
  • What is that? (point to schedule) → It’s a schedule.

7. Writing Practice

  • I’m going to the square tonight. – Voy a la plaza esta noche.
  • We’re in the museum on Wednesday. – Estamos en el museo el miércoles.
  • It’s a new week. – Es una semana nueva.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

  • Go to a museum tomorrow → I go to a museum tomorrow.
  • Say where you are on Saturday → I am in the square on Saturday.
