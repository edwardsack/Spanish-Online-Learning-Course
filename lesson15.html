Objective: Learn directional phrases (to the left) and combine with Where can I…? for navigation.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain directions for navigating cities (e.g., To the right). Show how Where can I…? (Day 11) combines with directions. Preview new directional vocabulary.

2. Grammar Focus – Directions

Directional Phrases:

  • to the right – a la derecha
  • to the left – a la izquierda
  • straight ahead – todo recto
  • near – cerca
  • far – lejos

Structure: Ask: Where can I + infinitive + article + noun? Example: Where can I buy a ticket? Answer: You can + infinitive + to the right/left. Example: You can buy a ticket to the right. Key Rule: Use a la for right/left (feminine nouns). Combine with to be able to for polite requests.

4. Listening Prompt

Say: You can buy a ticket to the right. The beach is straight ahead. Ask: Where can I buy a map? → Pause for: You can buy a map to the left. Goal: Recognize directions and to be able to.

5. Reading + Matching Task

Match: The station is to the right. →, You can buy a souvenir straight ahead. →, It’s a pretty street. →. Identify: verb, direction, noun in You can eat in the restaurant to the left.

6. Speaking Prompt

You: Where can I buy a ticket? → You can buy a ticket to the right.

You: “Say the map is far.” → The map is far.

You: What is that? (point to street) → It’s a street.

7. Writing Practice

Translate:

  • The museum is to the left. → El museo está a la izquierda.
  • You can buy a map straight ahead. → Puedes comprar un mapa todo recto.
  • The street is near. → La calle está cerca.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

You: “The park is to the right.” → The park is to the right.

You: “Ask where you can buy a map.” → Where can I buy a map?
