Day 10: Irregular Verb To Have + Possessions

Estimated Time: 25 minutes

Objective: Learn the irregular verb to have to talk about possessions, combining with numbers and travel nouns.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain to have for possessions (I have a ticket). Note to have is irregular. Preview: Using numbers and nouns (the suitcase).

2. Grammar Focus – To Have

PronounConjugationExample
YotengoI have a suitcase.
TútienesYou have a ticket.
Él/EllatieneShe has a beer.
NosotrostenemosWe have two euros.
Ellos/EllastienenThey have three tickets.

Structure: To have + number/article + noun (+ adjective).

Examples: I have two tickets – Tengo dos billetes. You have a new t-shirt – Tienes una camiseta nueva.

3. Vocabulary – Travel

SpanishEnglish
la bolsathe bag
el menúthe menu
la informaciónthe information

4. Listening Prompt

Say: I have two suitcases. You have a bag. Ask: How many tickets do you have? → Pause for: I have three tickets. Goal: Recognize to have and numbers.

5. Reading + Matching Task

Match:

  • I have three tickets → I have three tickets
  • She has a new bag → She has a new bag
  • We have five euros → We have five euros

6. Speaking Prompt

How many bags do you have? → I have two bags.
Say you have ten euros → I have ten euros.
Say what you buy → I buy a bag.

7. Writing Practice

Translate:

  • I have six bags → Tengo seis bolsas
  • We have four euros → Tenemos cuatro euros
  • She has seven tickets → Tiene siete billetes
  • Answers: Tengo ocho euros, Compro cinco camisetas

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

Ten euros → Ten euros.
Say you have five t-shirts → I have five t-shirts.
Say what you have and buy → I have two tickets and buy a bag.
