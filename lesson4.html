Day 4: Regular -ar Verbs – Buying and Speaking

Estimated Time: 30 minutes

Objective: Learn regular -ar verb conjugation with to buy and to speak for shopping and communication.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain that regular -ar verbs follow a predictable pattern, unlike is or there is. Show how to buy and to speak are useful for travelers (e.g., buying tickets, speaking Spanish). Preview conjugation and new nouns.

2. Grammar Focus – -ar Verbs

PronounComprar (to buy)Hablar (to speak)
Yocomprohablo
Túcomprashablas
Él/Ellacomprahabla
Nosotroscompramoshablamos
Ellos/Ellascompranhablan

Structure: Subject + conjugated verb + article + noun (+ adjective)

Examples:

  • Compro una maleta nueva. – I buy a new suitcase.
  • Hablas español. – You speak Spanish.

Key Rule: Drop -ar, add endings (-o, -as, -a, -amos, -an).

Task: Change I buy a beer to You buy a ticket.

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

Transcript:

  • Say: I buy a pretty t-shirt. You speak Spanish.
  • Ask: What do you buy? → Pause for: I buy a souvenir.

Goal: Recognize -ar verbs and nouns.

5. Reading + Matching Task

Match:

  • I buy a big souvenir
  • You speak Spanish
  • There is a new t-shirt

Identify: subject, verb, noun in I buy cold water.

6. Speaking Prompt

  • You say: What do you buy? → Student: I buy a t-shirt.
  • You say: “Say you speak Spanish.” → Student: I speak Spanish.
  • You say: What is that? (point to t-shirt) → Student: It’s a pretty t-shirt.

7. Writing Practice

Translate:

  • I buy a big souvenir. → Compro un souvenir grande.
  • You speak Spanish. → Hablas español.
  • She buys cold water. → Compra una agua fría.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

  • You say: “Buy a t-shirt.” → Student: I buy a t-shirt.
  • You say: “Describe what you buy.” → Student: I buy a new souvenir.
