Day 5: Regular -er Verbs – Eating and Drinking

Estimated Time: 30 minutes

Objective: Learn regular -er verb conjugation with to eat and to drink for dining scenarios.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain that -er verbs follow a different pattern than -ar verbs but are still regular. Show how to eat and to drink are key for ordering food/drinks. Preview conjugation and food nouns.

2. Grammar Focus – -er Verbs

PronounComer (to eat)Beber (to drink)
Yocomobebo
Túcomesbebes
Él/Ellacomebebe
Nosotroscomemosbebemos
Ellos/Ellascomenbeben

Structure: Subject + conjugated verb + article + noun (+ adjective)

Examples:

  • Como una cerveza fría. – I eat a cold beer.
  • Bebes agua. – You drink water.

Key Rule: Drop -er, add endings (-o, -es, -e, -emos, -en).

Task: Change I eat a salad to You drink a beer.

3. Vocabulary – Food/Drink

SpanishEnglish
la ensaladathe salad
el caféthe coffee
el vinothe wine
la comidathe food

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

Transcript:

  • Say: I eat a pretty salad. I drink a coffee.
  • Ask: What do you eat? → Pause for: I eat food.

Goal: Recognize -er verbs and food nouns.

5. Reading + Matching Task

Match:

  • I drink cold wine
  • I eat a new salad
  • There is a big coffee

Identify: subject, verb, noun in I drink a cold beer.

6. Speaking Prompt

  • You say: What do you drink? → Student: I drink wine.
  • You say: “Say you eat a salad.” → Student: I eat a salad.
  • You say: What is that? (point to coffee) → Student: It’s a coffee.

7. Writing Practice

Translate:

  • I eat a big salad. → Como una ensalada grande.
  • You drink a cold coffee. → Bebes un café frío.
  • We eat food. → Comemos comida.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

  • You say: “Eat a salad.” → Student: I eat a salad.
  • You say: “Describe what you drink.” → Student: I drink a cold beer.
