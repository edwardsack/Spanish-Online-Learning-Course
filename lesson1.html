Day 1: Articles — Your First Step to Speaking Spanish with Confidence

Estimated Time: 30 minutes

Objective: Understand and use Spanish definite and indefinite articles in real travel situations.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

  • Explain what articles are, how they differ from English, and why we start here.
  • Emphasize that every sentence begins with knowing the “what” and “how many.”

2. Grammar Focus – Articles: "The" and "A"

EnglishSpanish (Singular)Spanish (Plural)
the (masculine)el hotellos hoteles
the (feminine)la playalas playas
a / an (masculine)un taxiunos taxis
a / an (feminine)una cervezaunas cervezas

Key Rule: Articles match the gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural) of the noun.

  • el hotel ✅ (masculine, singular)
  • las playas ✅ (feminine, plural)

Mini-Rule: Most nouns ending in -o are masculine, -a are feminine, but exceptions exist (e.g., el mapa).

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

Transcript for your audio:

  • Say: “el hotel,” “la playa,” “una cerveza,” etc.
  • Ask: “¿Una habitación?” → Pause for student to repeat: Una habitación.

Goal: Associate article + noun by ear and repeat confidently.

5. Reading + Identification Exercise

  • Match the Spanish to the image: el hotel, la playa, una cerveza, el taxi, la maleta

6. Speaking Prompt

Simulated Hotel Check-In:

  • ¿Una habitación? → Una habitación.
  • ¿El pasaporte? → El pasaporte.
  • ¿Una maleta? → Una maleta.
  • ¿El hotel? → El hotel.

Goal: Practice pronunciation and article + noun structure.

7. Optional Writing Practice

Translate to Spanish:

  • The beach → La playa
  • A beer → Una cerveza
  • The tickets → Los billetes
  • A suitcase → Una maleta
  • The holidays → Las vacaciones

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

You hold up photos/flashcards (e.g., beach, hotel). Student responds aloud with correct article + noun (e.g., la playa).
