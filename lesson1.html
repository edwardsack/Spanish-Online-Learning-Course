Day 1: Articles — Your First Step to Speaking Spanish with Confidence

Estimated Time: 30 minutes

Objective: Understand and use Spanish definite and indefinite articles in real travel situations.

1. Intro Video Placeholder

  • Explain what articles are, how they differ from English, and why we start here.
  • Emphasize that every sentence begins with knowing the “what” and “how many.”

2. Grammar Focus – Articles

EnglishSpanish (Singular)Spanish (Plural)
the (masculine)el hotellos hoteles
the (feminine)la playalas playas
a / an (masculine)un taxiunos taxis
a / an (feminine)una cervezaunas cervezas

Key Rule: Articles match gender and number of the noun.

el hotel ✅ (masc, singular) — las playas ✅ (fem, plural)

3. Vocabulary – Travel Nouns

4. Listening Prompt

  • Say: “el hotel,” “la playa,” “una cerveza,”
  • Ask: “¿Una habitación?” → Pause → Student: Una habitación

5. Reading + Identification

Match the Spanish to the image: el hotel, la playa, una cerveza, el taxi, la maleta

6. Speaking Prompt

  • ¿Una habitación? → Una habitación.
  • ¿El pasaporte? → El pasaporte.
  • ¿Una maleta? → Una maleta.
  • ¿El hotel? → El hotel.

7. Writing Practice

  • The beach → La playa
  • A beer → Una cerveza
  • The tickets → Los billetes
  • A suitcase → Una maleta
  • The holidays → Las vacaciones

8. Recap Challenge

Show flashcards (e.g., beach, hotel). Student responds: la playa, el hotel, etc.
