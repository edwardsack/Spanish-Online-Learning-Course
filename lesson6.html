Day 6: Regular -ir Verbs – Living and Opening

Estimated Time: 30 minutes

Objective: Learn regular -ir verb conjugation with to live and to open for travel scenarios.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain that -ir verbs are regular but have slightly different endings than -er. Show how to live (e.g., I live in the city) and to open (e.g., I open the suitcase) are useful. Preview conjugation and hotel nouns.

2. Grammar Focus – -ir Verbs

PronounVivir (to live)Abrir (to open)
Yovivoabro

Structure: Subject + conjugated verb + article + noun (+ adjective)

Examples:

  • Vivo en una ciudad grande. – I live in a big city.
  • Abres una maleta nueva. – You open a new suitcase.

Key Rule: Drop -ir, add endings (-o, -es, -e, -imos, -en).

Task: Change I live in a city to You open a door.

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

  • Say: I live in a pretty city. I open a door.
  • Ask: What do you open? → Pause for: I open a suitcase.

Goal: Recognize -ir verbs and nouns.

5. Reading + Matching Task

Match:

  • I open a new key
  • I live in a big city
  • There is a small bathroom

Identify: subject, verb, noun in You open a new door.

6. Speaking Prompt

  • You say: Where do you live? → Student: I live in a city.
  • You say: “Say you open a door.” → I open a door.
  • You say: What is that? (point to key) → It’s a key.

7. Writing Practice

  • I open a new bathroom. → Abro un baño nuevo.
  • You live in a pretty city. → Vives en una ciudad bonita.
  • We open a reservation. → Abrimos una reserva.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

  • You say: “Open a key.” → Student: I open a key.
  • You say: “Describe where you live.” → I live in a big city.
