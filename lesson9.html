Day 9: Numbers 1–10 + Irregular Verb *To Cost*

Estimated Time: 25 minutes

Objective: Learn numbers 1–10 and use to cost (irregular) to ask "How much does it cost?" for shopping.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

  • Explain numbers 1–10 and "How much does it cost?" for buying souvenirs or tickets.
  • Note to cost is irregular, unlike to buy, to eat, or to live.
  • Preview: Asking prices and counting items.

Structure: Ask: "How much does + article + noun cost?"

Example: How much does the beer cost? → It costs/They cost + number + euros.

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

  • Say: "How much does the t-shirt cost? It costs five euros. I buy two tickets."
  • Ask: "How much does the beer cost?" → Pause for: "It costs three euros."
  • Goal: Recognize numbers, to cost, and shopping phrases.

5. Reading + Matching Task

  • Match "How much does the money cost?" → They cost seven euros.
  • Match "I buy three tickets."
  • Identify: question word, verb, noun in "How much does the t-shirt cost?"

6. Speaking Prompt

  • How much does the beer cost? → It costs five euros.
  • Ask about the price of a ticket → How much does the ticket cost?
  • Say what you buy → I buy two t-shirts.

7. Writing Practice

  • Translate: How much does the ticket cost? They cost nine euros. I buy four t-shirts.
  • Answers:
    • ¿Cuánto cuesta el billete?
    • Cuestan nueve euros.
    • Compro cuatro camisetas.
    • Cuesta seis euros.
    • Compro cinco billetes.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

  • “Seven euros” → Seven euros.
  • Ask about the price of a t-shirt → How much does the t-shirt cost?
  • Say you buy three tickets → I buy three tickets.
