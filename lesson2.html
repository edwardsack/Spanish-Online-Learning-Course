Day 2: Subject Pronouns — Talking About People

Estimated Time: 35 minutes

Objective: Learn to identify and use Spanish subject pronouns (I, you, he, she, we, they) in real conversations.

1. Intro Explanation (Video Placeholder)

Explain that subject pronouns tell us who is doing the action. In Spanish, these pronouns sometimes get dropped because verbs already show “who,” but learners must know them to understand and speak correctly.

2. Pronouns Table

Key Notes:

  • tú is informal; usted is formal (we’ll cover later).
  • Spain uses vosotros/vosotras, but in Latin America, ustedes is used for “you all.”

3. Listening & Repeat

You say each pronoun, student repeats. E.g., “yo… tú… él… ella… nosotros…”

4. Vocabulary in Context (Travel)

  • Yo tengo un pasaporte. (I have a passport.)
  • Tú tienes la maleta. (You have the suitcase.)
  • Él tiene un taxi. (He has a taxi.)
  • Ella tiene la habitación. (She has the room.)
  • Nosotros tenemos las vacaciones. (We have the holidays.)

5. Reading Exercise

Match the pronoun to the person:

  • yo → (picture of you)
  • ella → (picture of a woman)
  • nosotros → (group of friends)

6. Speaking Prompt

Practice questions:

  • ¿Quién tiene el pasaporte? → Yo tengo el pasaporte.
  • ¿Quién tiene la maleta? → Ella tiene la maleta.
  • ¿Quién tiene un taxi? → Él tiene un taxi.

7. Optional Writing Practice

Translate into Spanish:

  • We have the hotel. → Nosotros tenemos el hotel.
  • They (fem) have the beach. → Ellas tienen la playa.
  • You (informal) have the beer. → Tú tienes la cerveza.

8. Recap Challenge

Hold up a flashcard or object (e.g., suitcase). Student says a sentence with the correct pronoun:

“Yo tengo la maleta.” / “Ella tiene la maleta.”
