Day 11: Irregular Verb To Be Able To + Polite Requests

Estimated Time: 25 minutes

Objective: Learn the irregular verb to be able to for requests (Where can I buy…?) and add travel nouns.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain to be able to for polite requests (Where can I…?). Note it is irregular, unlike to buy, to eat, to live. Preview asking where to buy or do things.

2. Grammar Focus – To Be Able To

Pronoun Conjugation Example
YopuedoI can buy a t-shirt.
TúpuedesYou can eat food.
Él/EllapuedeShe can open the door.
NosotrospodemosWe can speak Spanish.
Ellos/EllaspuedenThey can buy tickets.

Structure: Where can I + infinitive + article + noun? Example: Where can I buy a t-shirt? Answer: You can + infinitive + in + article + noun.

4. Listening Prompt

Say: Where can I buy a souvenir? You can buy a souvenir in the shop. Ask: Where can I eat food? → Pause for: You can eat food in the restaurant.

5. Reading + Matching Task

Match sentences to context: Where can I buy a menu?, You can eat in the restaurant., I open the door.

6. Speaking Prompt

Practice: Where can I buy a ticket? → You can buy a ticket in the station. Ask where you can eat → Where can I eat food? Say what you can do → I can speak Spanish.

7. Writing Practice

Translate: Where can I open the door?, I can buy a ticket., You can eat in the market.

Answers:

  • ¿Dónde puedo abrir la puerta?
  • Puedo comprar un billete.
  • Puedes comer en el mercado.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

Practice: Where is the shop? → It’s there. Ask where you can buy a t-shirt → Where can I buy a t-shirt? Say what you can do in a restaurant → I can eat in the restaurant.
