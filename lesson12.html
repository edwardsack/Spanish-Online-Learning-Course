Day 12: Review Day – Dialogues with Regular and Irregular Verbs

Estimated Time: 30 minutes

Objective: Consolidate Days 1–11 with unscripted dialogues using regular (to buy, to eat, to open) and irregular verbs (to be, to want, to have, to cost, to be able to).

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain that today combines grammar and vocab into flexible travel dialogues. Highlight regular and irregular verbs. Preview: Hotel, restaurant, and shopping scenarios.

2. Grammar Focus – Review

  • Regular Verbs:
    • -ar: I buy, you buy
    • -er: I eat, you eat
    • -ir: I live, you live
  • Irregular Verbs:
    • To Be: I am, you are, is, we are, they are
    • To Want: I want, you want
    • To Have: I have, you have, has, we have, they have
    • To Cost: it costs, they cost
    • To Be Able To: I can, you can, he/she can, we can, they can

Tips: Combine verbs with nouns/adjectives. Use please for politeness.

3. Vocabulary – Review (No New Words)

Reuse: the hotel, the beer, the food, the souvenir, the t-shirt, the menu, the door. Phrases: What is that?, Where is…?, How much does it cost?, Where can I…?

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

Hotel: Do I open the door? – Yes, please.
Where can I have a menu? – You can have a menu in the restaurant.

Restaurant: Do you want to eat something? – Yes, I eat a salad, please.
How much does the beer cost? – It costs ten euros.

Shopping: Where can I buy a souvenir? – You can buy a souvenir in the shop.
I have five euros.
Ask: What do you buy? → I buy a t-shirt.

5. Reading + Dialogue Completion

  • Where ___ can I ___ a ticket? → can I buy
  • I eat ___ food ___ → in the restaurant, pretty
  • Does it cost ___ euros? → ten
  • Match:
    • I have a bag → I have a bag
    • Where can I eat? → Where can you eat?
    • It’s a big hotel → It’s a big hotel

6. Speaking Prompt

Hotel: You: Do you open the door? → Yes, I open the door.
You: Where can I have a menu? → You have a menu in the restaurant.

Restaurant: You: What do you eat? → I eat a salad, please.
You: How much does it cost? → It costs ten euros.

Shopping: You: Where can I buy a t-shirt? → You can buy a t-shirt in the shop.
You: Say what you have → I have five euros.

7. Writing Practice

  • Hotel: Open a door, ask for a menu.
  • Restaurant: Eat a salad, ask the price.
  • Shopping: Buy a t-shirt, say what you have.

Answers:

  • Hotel: Can I open the door? → Yes, please. Where can I have a menu? → You have a menu in the restaurant.
  • Restaurant: I eat a salad, please. How much does it cost? → It costs ten euros.
  • Shopping: Where can I buy a t-shirt? → It costs ten euros. I have two t-shirts.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

Role-Play: You (hotel): Who are you? → I am a tourist.
You (waiter): What do you want to eat? → I want a salad.
You (shopkeeper): How much does the souvenir cost? → It costs ten euros.
