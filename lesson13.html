Day 13: Irregular Verb To Go – Going Places

Estimated Time: 25 minutes

Objective: Learn the irregular verb to go to express travel plans, combined with destinations and prepositions (to, in).

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain that to go is irregular and used for going places (e.g., I go to the beach). Highlight its use with to or in for travel contexts. Preview new destination nouns.

2. Grammar Focus – To Go

Pronoun Conjugation Example
YovoyI go to the beach.
TúvasYou go to the restaurant.
Él/EllavaHe goes to the market.
NosotrosvamosWe go to the city.
Ellos/EllasvanThey go to the airport.

Structure: Subject + to go + to + article + noun (+ adjective). Example: I go to a pretty beach → Voy a una playa bonita.

Use in for general location: I am in the city (using to be from Day 11). Contraction: to + el = al (e.g., I go to the hotel). Key rule: Ir is irregular. Task: Change I go to the beach to You go to the market.

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

Say: I go to a pretty square. You go to the museum. Ask: Where do you go? → Pause for: I go to the park.

5. Reading + Matching Task

  • I go to a big mountain →
  • They go to the museum →
  • It’s a pretty square →
  • Identify: subject, verb, destination in We go to a new city

6. Speaking Prompt

Where do you go? → I go to the beach.
Say you’re going to a park → I go to a park.
What is that? (point to square) → It’s a pretty square.

7. Writing Practice

Translate:

  • I’m going to a big museum → Voy a un museo grande.
  • You’re going to the square → Vas a la plaza.
  • They’re in a new city → Están en una ciudad nueva.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

Go to a mountain → I go to a mountain.
Say where you are → I am in a park.
