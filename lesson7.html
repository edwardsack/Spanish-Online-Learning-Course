Day 7: Irregular Verb "To Want" – Expressing Wants

Estimated Time: 25 minutes

Objective: Learn the irregular verb to want to express desires in travel contexts.

1. Intro Video Placeholder (You)

Explain that to want is irregular, unlike to buy, to eat, or to live. Show its use: I want a beer (e.g., Quiero una cerveza). Preview: Combine with food, shopping, and politeness.

2. Grammar Focus – To Want

PronounConjugation

Túquieres
Él/Ellaquiere
Nosotrosqueremos

Structure: I want + article + noun (+ adjective) + please.

Examples:

  • Quiero una cerveza fría, por favor. – I want a cold beer, please.
  • Quieres un café. – You want a coffee.

Key Rule: Querer doesn’t follow regular patterns (e.g., I buy vs. I want). Add please for politeness.

Task: Change I want a ticket to You want a t-shirt.

4. Listening Prompt (You Provide)

  • Say: I want a salad, please. I want the bill.
  • Ask: What do you want? → Pause for: I want a coffee.

Goal: Recognize to want and politeness.

5. Reading + Matching Task

Match:

  • I want a new ticket
  • I want the bill, please
  • I eat pretty food

Identify: verb, article, noun in I want a cold beer.

6. Speaking Prompt

  • You say: What do you want to eat? → Student: I want a salad, please.
  • You say: “Ask for a bill.” → I want the bill, please.
  • You: What is that? (point to ticket) → It’s a ticket.

7. Writing Practice

  • I want a new ticket, please. → Quiero un boleto nuevo, por favor.
  • You want cold water. → Quieres una agua fría.
  • We want the bill. → Queremos la cuenta.

8. Recap Challenge (Speaking)

  • You say: Do you want to eat something? → I want food, please.
  • You: “Say what you want to buy.” → I want a t-shirt.
